Group Processes

The group worked cohesively for Assessment 2 and brought the same can do attitudes and work ethic into Assessment 3. One change that was made was a stronger review process of work that wasnt our own. What this meant was empowering each individual team member to make changes as they saw fit to work that someone else had committed. Due to the work behind committed regularly to the repository, any changes were able to be seen in the blame log and either challenged or supported as required.

Another change was our approach to team meetings. During A2 we elected to share the Role of Chair and scheduling around the group, which allowed different members to experience the roll, but also lead to uncertainty and inconsistent structures for Agendas and Actions. For A3 Rory scheduled a weekly recurring meeting for Wednesdays after our course Tutorial. He chaired the meetings and also managed the agendas, meeting minutes, action items and any additional meetings that needed to be scheduled.

With one group member sadly having to move on to other things, the group was able step it up another level and divide the workload amongst the remaining members based on their own individual stengths displayed from Assessmen 2. The result was work delivered to the same standard within the time constraints of the project and life outside.
